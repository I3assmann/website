Jake Steven Zimmermann Simmons | University of Toronto | jake.zimmermann@mail.utoronto.ca

I am Jake Steven Zimmermann Simmons. I am a PhD student in mathematics at the University of Toronto. I am currently researching under Dr.Peter Dukes and I am really enjoying it. I was able to do this research because I won the Student Undergraduate Research Award (SURA), competing against students from the whole UVic science faculty. It finished at the end of July but I will be able to continue it with a Jamie Cassels Undergraduate Research Award (JCURA) throughout the next year.

We are looking at a cone in n(n-1)/2 real dimensions where each dimension corresponds to an edge weight in the complete graph on n vertices. Specifically we are looking at the cone generated from all positive linear combinations of all triangle vectors (i.e the corresponding graph is a triangle). We are looking at the facets of this cone, establishing an adjacency between them, and studying it to look for possible recursion methods on the cone.

At the moment my mathematical interests are very broad. I am a huge fan of algebra and analysis and am very excited to learn some topology in the coming year. I really love playing with infinity. I also enjoy combinatorics and graph theory.

Recently I attended the Canadian Undergraduate Mathematics Conference (CUMC 2016) at UVic, for which I was on the organising committee. It was a really fantastic experience for me. I feel it inspired me and invigorated my passion for math, as is often the case when I spend time around driven passionate people.

My other interests include music, world building, basketball, and water polo.
